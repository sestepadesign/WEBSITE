DESCRIPTION & SPECIFICATIONS:
- In the Crestatx garden, we have undergone a significant transformation by replacing the lawn with a carefully planned design. We have opted for mini-sized river stones to facilitate circulation, combined with a selection of strategically placed plants to create a harmonious visual balance.
- At the entrance, grasses and salvias predominate, offering a natural and fresh welcome. Around the pool, we have opted for shrubby species inspired by the tropics, such as Cycads, Bird of Paradise, Ficus, and Pittosporum.
- Concept and Transformation In the Crestatx garden, we replaced the lawn with a carefully planned design. We chose mini-sized river stones to facilitate circulation. This material combines with strategically placed plants to create a harmonious visual balance.
- Entrance and Pool Area Grasses and salvias predominate at the entrance, offering a natural and fresh welcome. Around the pool, we selected shrubby species inspired by the tropics. This palette includes Cycads, Bird of Paradise, Ficus, and Pittosporum.
- Vegetation Islands In the rear part of the garden, we created vegetation islands that lead towards the side area. Here, we used aromatic and creeping plants along with selected trees. These elements integrate the vegetation with the surrounding gravel organically and attractively.
- We also highlight a wooded area bordered by ribbon-like plants such as Ophiopogon and Agapanthus. Shrubs like Myrtles blend perfectly into this environment. Furthermore, we elegantly framed this space using corten steel to create a charming contrast with the lush vegetation.
- Light and Shade Management We clearly differentiated the shaded forest area from the sun-exposed gravel area. In the shaded sections, we chose species adapted to low-light conditions, such as Ophiopogon and Chlorophytum. These plants add texture and color even to the darkest corners of the garden.
- Sustainability and Purpose In summary, our main focus was to reduce water consumption by eliminating the lawn. At the same time, we created pedestrian pathways among the plants to allow for the full enjoyment of this green space.
