01

Ignoring Local Water Restrictions

One of the most common mistakes new estate owners make is trying to replicate Northern European aesthetics in the Mediterranean. Mallorca faces severe water shortages during the summer. Designing a garden heavily reliant on irrigation is not only environmentally irresponsible, but it guarantees exorbitant water bills and a constant struggle to keep plants alive.

The S'Estepa Solution

We champion "Dry Gardening" and Mediterranean-native planting. By selecting drought-tolerant species like Olive, Mastic, and Rosemary, we create lush, textured landscapes that thrive naturally in the Balearic climate.

S'Estepa Design Studio 02 / 04
02

High-Maintenance Planting

A holiday villa is meant for relaxation, not weekend labor. Intricate flower beds and fast-growing exotics require constant pruning, weeding, and feeding. When you arrive in Mallorca for your summer holiday, the last thing you want is a garden that looks overgrown and neglected.

The S'Estepa Solution

We design structural, low-maintenance gardens using architectural plants, gravel surfaces, and slow-growing Mediterranean shrubs. Your garden will look manicured year-round with minimal intervention.

S'Estepa Design Studio 03 / 04
03

Generic Lawns Instead of Gravel Gardens

Expansive green lawns look out of place in the Mallorcan countryside and require thousands of liters of water, constant mowing, and chemical fertilizers. They are the antithesis of luxury Mediterranean living and often struggle in the August heat.

The S'Estepa Solution

We replace thirsty grass with elegant, locally-sourced gravels (like Marés stone chippings), creating sophisticated, usable outdoor spaces that seamlessly blend your villa with the natural Tramuntana landscape.
