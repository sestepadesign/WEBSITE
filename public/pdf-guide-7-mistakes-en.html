7 Expensive Landscaping Mistakes
to Avoid in Mallorca

01

The "English Lawn" Fallacy

Imposing vast expanses of traditional turf in Mallorca's climate is a fundamental design error. It requires exorbitant volumes of water, constant chemical fertilization, and daily maintenance, resulting in an artificial landscape constantly battling its environment.

THE S'ESTEPA SOLUTION

We replace generic lawns with endemic groundcovers (like Lippia nodiflora) or design sweeping architectural gravel and natural stone hardscaping, interspersed with wild Mediterranean vegetation.

02

Ignoring Island Microclimates

Assuming the entire island shares the same conditions is negligent. A project in the Tramuntana mountains faces saline winds, winter frosts, and rocky soils—conditions drastically different from the protected, arid interior.

THE S'ESTEPA SOLUTION

We conduct hyper-local botanical analysis prior to design. We select species that don't just survive, but naturally thrive in the exact coordinates of your estate, guaranteeing a mature and resilient landscape.

S'Estepa Design Studio 03 / 08
03

Disregarding Soil Architecture

Planting high-value flora in poor or poorly drained substrate is a guaranteed loss of investment. The heavy clay or excessively calcareous soil found in certain areas of Mallorca will quickly suffocate the roots of unadapted species.

THE S'ESTEPA SOLUTION

Advanced soil engineering. Before any planting occurs, we structure the terrain to ensure perfect drainage and amend the earth with specific organic matter, replicating the optimal conditions of the Mediterranean forest.

04

Overlooking Vertical Integration

In luxury commercial projects or terraced hillside villas, treating retaining walls as dead space is a wasted opportunity. Leaving them bare increases structural temperature and breaks visual immersion.

THE S'ESTEPA SOLUTION

Implementation of advanced engineering Vertical Gardens and fluid terrace design. We transform concrete barriers into living canvases that improve acoustics, thermal regulation, and offer a cutting-edge aesthetic.

S'Estepa Design Studio 05 / 08
05

Planting Non-Native Species

Importing exotic species from tropical climates exposes them to local plagues, such as the infamous Red Palm Weevil or Xylella fastidiosa. This is not only an aesthetic risk, but a biological liability for the property.

THE S'ESTEPA SOLUTION

A curated catalog of endemic and adapted species (ancient Olive trees, Pistacia lentiscus, Rosmarinus prostratus). These species possess natural immunity to local pests and require a fraction of the water.

06

The "Summer-Only" Aesthetic

Designing a garden that only reaches its visual peak during July and August. When autumn or winter arrives, the property is stripped of color, texture, and structure, looking desolate and neglected.

THE S'ESTEPA SOLUTION

Strategic seasonal design (Four-Season Architecture). We select a botanical matrix that guarantees continuous visual interest: silver evergreen foliage for winter, early spring blooms, and textured barks.

07

Cheapening Hardscape Materials

Using generic treated woods, low-quality porous tiles, or inconsistent gravels that degrade, discolor, or accumulate mold under Mallorca's sun and humidity. Inferior materials immediately ruin the perception of luxury.

THE S'ESTEPA SOLUTION

Uncompromising selection of noble materials. We utilize traditional Mallorcan dry stone, premium travertine, corten steel, and certified tropical woods that age with dignity and acquire an elegant patina.
